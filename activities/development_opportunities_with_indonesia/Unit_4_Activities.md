# Warm-up Activities

## 1. Transport Route Brainstorm
- Display a large map of Timor-Leste and NTT
- In pairs, students identify and draw potential transport routes between the two regions
- Pairs share their ideas with the class, discussing potential challenges for each route

## 2. Border Crossing Bingo
- Create bingo cards with common border crossing items/procedures
- Read out scenarios, and students mark off items on their cards
- First to get a line wins and explains the importance of each item marked

# Main Lesson Activities

## 1. Transport Infrastructure Gallery Walk
- Set up stations around the room with information on different transport modes
- Students rotate through stations, taking notes on current state and challenges
- Class discussion to synthesize findings

## 2. Trade Facilitation Jigsaw
- Divide class into expert groups, each focusing on a different best practice
- Experts then form new groups to share their knowledge
- Groups create posters summarizing all best practices

# Group Work Tasks

## 1. Transport Corridor Improvement Project
- Assign groups a specific transport corridor between Timor-Leste and NTT
- Groups analyze current state, challenges, and propose improvements
- Present findings in a mock stakeholder meeting format

## 2. Border Procedure Optimization Challenge
- Provide groups with a detailed current border crossing procedure
- Challenge them to streamline the process, reducing time and complexity
- Groups present their optimized procedures, justifying changes

# Individual Practice Exercises

## 1. Trade Facilitation Measure Analysis
- Students choose one trade facilitation measure from a provided list
- Individually research and write a one-page analysis of its potential impact
- Share findings in a brief presentation to a small group

## 2. Customs Declaration Form Completion
- Provide students with a complex scenario and blank customs declaration form
- Students complete the form based on the scenario
- Peer review for accuracy and completeness

# Cool-down Activities

## 1. One-Minute Paper
- Students write for one minute on:
  1. The most important thing they learned
  2. One question they still have
- Collect papers to inform future lessons and address remaining questions

## 2. Transport and Trade Facilitation Pictionary
- Create cards with key terms from the lesson
- Students take turns drawing while others guess the term
- Briefly review the importance of each term as it's guessed correctly
