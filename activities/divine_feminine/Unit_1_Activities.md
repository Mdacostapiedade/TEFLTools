# Warm-up Activities

## Anonymous Question Box
- Students write anonymous questions about human sexuality on index cards
- Instructor collects, shuffles, and reads aloud, facilitating brief class discussions

## Two Truths and a Lie: Sexuality Edition
- Students write three statements about human sexuality, two true and one false
- In small groups, students guess which statement is false for each person

# Main Lesson Activities

## Jigsaw Reading
- Divide class into groups, each assigned a different aspect of sexuality (biological, psychological, social)
- Groups read and summarize their assigned topic, then teach other groups

## Evolutionary Theory Debate
- Divide class into two groups: one supporting evolutionary theories of human mating, one challenging them
- Groups prepare arguments and engage in a structured debate

# Group Work or Pair Work Tasks

## Cultural Influences Mind Map
- In pairs, create a mind map showing how culture influences views on sexuality
- Share and compare mind maps with other pairs

## Case Study Analysis
- Small groups analyze case studies of individuals from different cultures
- Identify how biological, psychological, and social factors shape their sexuality

# Individual Practice Exercises

## Personal Reflection Journal
- Students write a reflective journal entry on how their personal experiences and cultural background have shaped their views on sexuality

## Vocabulary Crossword
- Complete a crossword puzzle using key terms from the lesson

# Cool-down or Wrap-up Activities

## One-Minute Paper
- Students write for one minute on the most important thing they learned and one question they still have

## Sexuality Spectrum
- Create a large spectrum on the floor with tape
- Students position themselves on the spectrum based on their agreement with various statements about sexuality, discussing their positions

## Concept Snowball
- Students write one key concept from the lesson on a piece of paper
- Crumple papers into "snowballs" and toss around the room
- Each student picks up a snowball, reads the concept, and adds a related idea before tossing again
