# Warm-up Activities

## 1. Consciousness Observation Exercise (10 minutes)
- Students sit comfortably with eyes closed
- Guide them to observe their thoughts, sensations, and emotions without judgment
- After 5 minutes, ask them to slowly open their eyes
- Pair-share: Discuss observations with a partner

## 2. Reality vs. Illusion Quick Draw (5 minutes)
- Provide students with paper and colored pencils
- Ask them to quickly draw two images: one representing "reality" and one representing "illusion"
- Gallery walk: Students view each other's drawings and discuss interpretations

# Main Lesson Activities

## 1. Brahman Exploration Web (15 minutes)
- Create a large web diagram on the board with "Brahman" at the center
- Students contribute ideas, characteristics, and associations related to Brahman
- Discuss and refine the web as a class, adding key concepts from Yoga Vasishta

## 2. Consciousness Creation Roleplay (20 minutes)
- Divide class into small groups
- Each group creates a short skit demonstrating how consciousness shapes perceived reality
- Groups perform skits for the class, followed by brief discussions

# Group Work or Pair Work Tasks

## 1. Maya in Daily Life Scavenger Hunt (25 minutes)
- In pairs, students list examples of maya (illusion) in their daily lives
- Pairs join to form groups of four and compare lists
- Groups create posters illustrating their top 5 examples of maya
- Present posters to the class

## 2. Timorese Beliefs vs. Yoga Vasishta Venn Diagram (20 minutes)
- In small groups, create a Venn diagram comparing traditional Timorese beliefs with Yoga Vasishta teachings
- Groups share their diagrams, discussing similarities and differences

# Individual Practice Exercises

## 1. Personal Reality Reflection (15 minutes)
- Students write a reflective essay on how their consciousness shapes their personal reality
- Prompt: "Describe a situation where your perception changed, altering your experience of reality"

## 2. Maya Identification Journal (10 minutes)
- Provide students with a template for a week-long journal
- Students record daily instances where they recognize maya in their lives
- Encourage them to note how this awareness affects their thoughts and actions

# Cool-down or Wrap-up Activities

## 1. Consciousness Ripple Effect (10 minutes)
- Students stand in a circle
- One student starts by sharing a key takeaway from the lesson
- The next student builds on this idea, creating a "ripple effect" of understanding
- Continue until all students have contributed

## 2. Three-Word Summary (5 minutes)
- Challenge students to summarize their understanding of the lesson in exactly three words
- Students write their summaries on sticky notes
- Create a "word wall" with all the summaries, grouping similar concepts together
- Briefly discuss emerging patterns and key themes
