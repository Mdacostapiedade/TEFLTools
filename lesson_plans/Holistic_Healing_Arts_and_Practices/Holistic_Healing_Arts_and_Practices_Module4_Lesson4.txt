Lesson Objectives:
- Students will learn vocabulary related to mindfulness and meditation
- Students will understand the basic principles and benefits of mindfulness meditation
- Students will practice simple mindfulness techniques for stress reduction and self-awareness
- Students will reflect on their own experiences with mindfulness and its potential applications in their lives

Warm-up (10 mins):
- Ask students to share any experiences they have with meditation or mindfulness
- Discuss the concept of mindfulness as paying attention to the present moment without judgment

Vocabulary Introduction (20 mins):
- Present key terms related to mindfulness and meditation, such as:
  - Mindfulness, awareness, presence, non-judgmental
  - Meditation, focus, attention, distraction
  - Breath, body, thoughts, emotions, sensations

- Provide definitions and examples for each word in the context of mindfulness practice
- Have students practice pronouncing and using the new vocabulary in sentences

Reading and Discussion (30 mins):
- Have the students read Tibetan Meditation practice in the Readings folder.

- Read the passage aloud and check for understanding
- Have students take turns reading sections of the passage
- Discuss the main ideas and answer any questions that arise

Demonstration and Practice (30 mins):
- Guide students through a mindfulness meditation, such as a body scan or mindful breathing exercise
- Provide clear instructions and demonstrations for the exercise
- Emphasize the importance of finding a comfortable posture, focusing on the present moment, and allowing thoughts and sensations to come and go without judgment
- Have students practice the exercise for a few minutes, then pause to check in and share their experiences

Reflection and Discussion (30 mins):
- Ask students to reflect on their experience with the mindfulness exercise and any challenges or insights that arose
- Discuss the potential benefits and applications of mindfulness in daily life, such as for stress management, communication, or creativity
- Explore how cultural attitudes and beliefs may influence perceptions of mindfulness and meditation
- Encourage students to share their own strategies for cultivating mindfulness and well-being

Wrap-up (10 mins):
- Review the key vocabulary and concepts from the lesson
- Emphasize the value of regular mindfulness practice for personal growth and resilience
- Provide resources for further learning about mindfulness and meditation techniques

Homework:
- Ask students to practice the mindfulness exercise learned in class for at least 5 minutes a day and keep a journal of their observations
- Have them write a short reflection on how mindfulness could be applied to a specific area of their life, such as relationships or studies
- Invite students to share their reflections and experiences at the next class
