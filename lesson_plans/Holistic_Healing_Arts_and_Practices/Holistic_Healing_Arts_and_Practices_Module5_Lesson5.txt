Lesson Objectives:
- Students will learn vocabulary related to prayer, ritual, and spiritual healing practices
- Students will understand the role of spirituality in health and well-being
- Students will explore the Catholic tradition and indigenous healing practices in Timor Leste
- Students will reflect on their own experiences with prayer, ritual, or spiritual practices in healing

Warm-up (10 mins):
- Show images of people engaging in prayer, ritual, or spiritual practices from different cultures
- Ask students to share any experiences they have with using prayer or spirituality for healing

Vocabulary Introduction (20 mins):
- Present key terms related to prayer, ritual, and spiritual healing, such as:
  - Prayer, intention, devotion, faith
  - Ritual, ceremony, offering, sacred
  - Healing, wholeness, harmony, divine

- Provide definitions and examples for each word in the context of healing practices
- Have students practice pronouncing and using the new vocabulary in sentences

Jigsaw Reading and Editing Activity (40 mins):
- Divide the class into four groups and assign each group a short reading on one of the following topics and have them correct the generic readings from an outsiders perspective with what THEY know:
  1. The role of prayer and the Catholic faith in health and healing in Timor Leste
  2. Traditional healing practices of the Tetum people
  3. Healing rituals and beliefs of the Mambai people
  4. Spiritual healing practices of the Fataluku people

- Have each group read and discuss their assigned topic, identifying key points and vocabulary
- Rearrange the groups so that each new group has one member from each of the original topic groups
- Have students take turns sharing what they learned about their topic with their new group members

- Bring the class back together and have each group share a summary of what they learned

Reflection and Discussion (30 mins):
- Ask students to reflect on their own experiences with prayer, ritual, or spiritual practices in healing
- Encourage them to consider how their cultural or family background has influenced their beliefs about spirituality and health
- Have students share their reflections with a partner or small group
- Invite volunteers to share insights or questions with the whole class
- Discuss how an understanding of diverse spiritual perspectives can enhance cross-cultural communication and empathy

Wrap-up (10 mins):
- Review the key vocabulary and concepts from the lesson
- Emphasize the importance of respecting and appreciating diverse spiritual traditions in healing
- Provide resources for further learning about the Catholic tradition and indigenous healing practices in Timor Leste

Homework:
- Ask students to interview a family member or friend about their experiences with prayer, ritual, or spiritual practices in healing
- Have them write a short reflection comparing and contrasting their own beliefs with what they learned from the interview
- Invite students to share their reflections at the next class

## Grammar Points:

- Use of prepositions to describe spiritual practices:
  - "Pray for healing" vs. "Pray to a deity"
  - "Believe in the power of prayer" vs. "Believe on faith alone"
  - "Participate in a ritual" vs. "Engage with a spiritual practice"

- Present perfect tense to discuss experiences with spiritual healing:
  - "I have prayed for healing before."
  - "She has participated in traditional rituals."
  - "They have never experienced spiritual healing."

- Conditional sentences to express beliefs and hypothetical situations:
  - "If I were ill, I would seek spiritual healing."
  - "Had they known about the ritual, they might have participated."

- Comparative and superlative forms to discuss different healing practices:
  - "Prayer is more common than ritual offerings in some cultures."
  - "The most important aspect of spiritual healing is faith."

- Passive voice to describe rituals and practices:
  - "Offerings are made to the spirits."
  - "Prayers are said for the sick."
  - "Healing ceremonies are performed by shamans."
