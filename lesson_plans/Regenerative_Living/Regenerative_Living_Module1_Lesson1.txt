# Lesson Plan: Foundations of Permaculture - Ethics and Principles

## Objectives:
- Students will be able to explain the three core ethics of permaculture.
- Students will be able to identify and describe the twelve principles of permaculture design.
- Students will be able to apply the ethics and principles to real-world scenarios.

## Duration: 90 minutes

## Materials:
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handouts with permaculture ethics and principles (see resources)
- Case study scenarios for group discussion

## Warm-up (10 minutes):
- Introduce the topic of permaculture and ask students to share any prior knowledge or experiences.
- Write the word "permaculture" on the board and brainstorm related words and concepts as a class.

## Presentation (30 minutes):
- Explain the three core ethics of permaculture: Earth Care, People Care, and Fair Share.
- Provide examples of how each ethic can be applied in real-life situations.
- Introduce the twelve principles of permaculture design, using visual aids and examples for each principle.
- Encourage students to ask questions and share their thoughts on the ethics and principles.

## Practice (30 minutes):
- Divide the class into small groups and assign each group a case study scenario.
- Ask the groups to discuss how the permaculture ethics and principles could be applied to their scenario.
- Have each group present their findings to the class and encourage class discussion.

## Production (15 minutes):
- Ask students to individually write a short reflection on how they can apply permaculture ethics and principles in their own lives.
- Encourage students to share their reflections with a partner or small group.

## Wrap-up (5 minutes):
- Review the main points of the lesson and answer any remaining questions.
- Assign homework: ask students to observe their local environment and identify examples of permaculture ethics and principles in action.

## Extension:
- Have students create a visual representation (e.g., poster, infographic) of the permaculture ethics and principles to display in the classroom or share with the community.
