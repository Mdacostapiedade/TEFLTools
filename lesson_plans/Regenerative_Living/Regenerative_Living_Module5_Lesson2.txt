Lesson Plan: Natural Beekeeping in Timor-Leste

Resources:
See Readings folder

Duration: 120 minutes

Objectives:
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Understand the importance of bees in Timorese agriculture and ecosystems
2. Identify local bee species and their characteristics
3. Design and manage natural hives suitable for Timor-Leste's climate
4. Recognize common health issues in bees and learn preventive measures
5. Understand the production and harvesting of bee products
6. Appreciate the connection between beekeeping and Timorese traditions

Materials:
- Whiteboard and markers
- Handouts with diagrams of natural hives and bee anatomy
- Pictures of local bee species and flowering plants
- Samples of local bee products (if available)
- Materials for hive model construction (wood, bamboo, palm leaves)
- Protective gear for demonstration

I. Introduction (15 minutes)
   A. The role of bees in Timor-Leste's ecosystems and agriculture
   B. Brief history of beekeeping in Timor-Leste
   C. Importance of bees in local culture and traditions

II. Local Bee Species (20 minutes)
    A. Apis cerana (Asian honey bee)
    B. Apis dorsata (Giant honey bee)
    C. Tetragonula species (Stingless bees)
    D. Discuss characteristics and behavior of each species

III. Natural Hive Design (30 minutes)
     A. Principles of natural beekeeping from "Keeping Bees with a Smile"
     B. Adapting hive designs to Timor-Leste's tropical climate
        1. Ventilation considerations
        2. Protection from rain and heat
        3. Using local materials (bamboo, palm leaves, local woods)
     C. Hands-on Activity: Design a natural hive (15 minutes)
        - Students work in groups to design a hive suitable for Timorese conditions

IV. Hive Management (20 minutes)
    A. Seasonal management in Timor-Leste's climate
    B. Minimal intervention approach
    C. Swarm management and natural reproduction
    D. Integrating beekeeping with local agroforestry practices

V. Bee Health (15 minutes)
   A. Common health issues in Timor-Leste (e.g., varroa mites, small hive beetles)
   B. Preventive measures aligned with natural beekeeping
      1. Promoting bee-friendly environments
      2. Using resistant local bee strains
   C. Traditional Timorese remedies for bee ailments

VI. Bee Products and Harvesting (15 minutes)
    A. Honey production and harvesting techniques
    B. Beeswax and its uses in Timorese culture
    C. Propolis and its medicinal properties
    D. Sustainable harvesting practices

VII. Bees in Timorese Culture (10 minutes)
     A. Traditional honey hunting practices
     B. Use of bee products in local medicine and rituals
     C. Beekeeping as a sustainable livelihood option

VIII. Conclusion and Discussion (10 minutes)
      A. Recap key points about natural beekeeping in Timor-Leste
      B. Discuss potential for expanding beekeeping in local communities
      C. Address any questions from students

IX. Assignment
    Students to design a bee-friendly garden using local flowering plants. The design should include:
    - A list of suitable plants that flower at different times of the year
    - A layout plan for the garden
    - Explanation of how the garden supports bees and other pollinators
    - Ideas for integrating the garden with traditional Timorese agriculture
