Lesson Plan: Community Build Projects in Timor-Leste

Duration: 120 minutes

Objectives:
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Understand the concept and importance of community build projects
2. Identify traditional Timorese communal building practices
3. Recognize the stages of planning and executing a community build project
4. Understand the benefits and challenges of community builds in Timor-Leste
5. Design a simple community build project proposal

Materials:
- Whiteboard and markers
- Pictures of successful community build projects in Timor-Leste or similar contexts
- Handouts with project planning templates
- Large paper and colored markers for group activity
- Case study materials of a local community build project

I. Introduction (15 minutes)
   A. Define community build projects
   B. Brief history of communal building practices in Timor-Leste
   C. Importance of community builds in rural development and nation-building

II. Traditional Timorese Communal Building Practices (20 minutes)
    A. Uma Lulik (sacred house) construction
    B. Gotong royong (mutual assistance) concept
    C. Traditional roles and responsibilities in communal builds
    D. Rituals and ceremonies associated with community building

III. Stages of a Community Build Project (25 minutes)
     A. Community needs assessment
     B. Project planning and design
        1. Identifying resources (human, material, financial)
        2. Creating a timeline
        3. Assigning roles and responsibilities
     C. Mobilizing the community
     D. Construction phase
     E. Project handover and maintenance

IV. Benefits of Community Build Projects (15 minutes)
    A. Strengthening community bonds
    B. Skill development and knowledge transfer
    C. Cost-effectiveness
    D. Increased sense of ownership and pride
    E. Preservation of traditional building techniques

V. Challenges and Solutions (15 minutes)
   A. Limited resources and funding
   B. Diverse skill levels within the community
   C. Balancing traditional practices with modern requirements
   D. Ensuring long-term maintenance
   E. Group discussion: Brainstorm solutions to these challenges

VI. Case Study Analysis (15 minutes)
    A. Present a case study of a successful community build project in Timor-Leste
    B. Analyze the project's planning, execution, and outcomes
    C. Discuss lessons learned and best practices

VII. Hands-on Activity: Design a Community Build Project (25 minutes)
     A. Divide students into groups
     B. Each group designs a proposal for a small community build project
        (e.g., community center, school kitchen, public toilet)
     C. Groups present their proposals, explaining the project goals, design, and community involvement plan

VIII. Conclusion and Discussion (10 minutes)
      A. Recap key points about community build projects in Timor-Leste
      B. Discuss potential for implementing more community builds
      C. Address any questions from students

IX. Assignment
    Students to research and document a recent or ongoing community build project in their area. The report should include:
    - Project description and goals
    - Community involvement process
    - Challenges faced and how they were overcome
    - Outcomes and community impact
    - Suggestions for future improvements
    - Photos or sketches of the project
