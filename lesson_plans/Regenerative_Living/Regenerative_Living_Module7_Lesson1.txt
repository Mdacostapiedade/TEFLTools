Lesson Plan: Fermentation Techniques in Timor-Leste - Kombucha, Kimchi, and Cider

Resources: See Readings and activities
Duration: 120 minutes

Objectives:
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Understand the basic principles of fermentation and its benefits
2. Identify local ingredients in Timor-Leste suitable for fermentation
3. Describe the process of making kombucha, kimchi, and cider
4. Recognize the health benefits and cultural significance of fermented foods
5. Demonstrate basic fermentation techniques

Materials:
- Whiteboard and markers
- Samples of fermented foods (if available)
- Pictures of fermentation processes and equipment
- Basic fermentation equipment (jars, cloths, weights)
- Ingredients for demonstration (tea, sugar, vegetables, apples or other local fruits)
- Handouts with recipe guidelines
- pH strips (if available)

I. Introduction (15 minutes)
   A. Define fermentation and its role in food preservation
   B. Brief history of fermentation in Timor-Leste and Southeast Asia
   C. Importance of fermentation in food security and nutrition

II. Principles of Fermentation (20 minutes)
    A. Microbial action in fermentation
    B. Anaerobic vs. aerobic fermentation
    C. Importance of salt, sugar, and acidity
    D. Safety considerations in home fermentation

III. Local Ingredients for Fermentation in Timor-Leste (15 minutes)
    A. Vegetables: cabbage, carrots, green papaya, chili peppers
    B. Fruits: papaya, mango, pineapple
    C. Tea: locally available black or green tea
    D. Sweeteners: coconut sugar, palm sugar, honey

IV. Kombucha Fermentation (20 minutes)
    A. What is kombucha?
    B. Ingredients and equipment needed
    C. Step-by-step process
    D. Flavoring options using local fruits
    E. Demonstration: Starting a kombucha batch

V. Kimchi-style Fermentation (20 minutes)
   A. Traditional kimchi vs. Timorese adaptation
   B. Ingredients and equipment needed
   C. Step-by-step process
   D. Variations using local vegetables
   E. Demonstration: Preparing vegetables for fermentation

VI. Cider Fermentation (20 minutes)
    A. Principles of fruit fermentation
    B. Adapting cider techniques for local fruits (e.g., papaya or pineapple "cider")
    C. Equipment and ingredients needed
    D. Step-by-step process
    E. Safety considerations for alcohol fermentation

VII. Health Benefits and Cultural Integration (10 minutes)
     A. Nutritional benefits of fermented foods
     B. Potential role in improving gut health
     C. Incorporating fermented foods into Timorese cuisine
     D. Economic potential of small-scale fermentation businesses

VIII. Hands-on Activity (20 minutes)
      A. Divide students into three groups
      B. Each group starts a small batch of one fermented product
      C. Groups explain their process and expected outcomes

IX. Conclusion and Discussion (10 minutes)
    A. Recap key points about fermentation
    B. Discuss potential for expanding fermentation practices in Timor-Leste
    C. Address any questions from students

X. Assignment
   Students to experiment with fermenting a local ingredient not covered in class. The report should include:
   - Chosen ingredient and reasoning
   - Fermentation process used
   - Observations during fermentation
   - Final product description (appearance, taste, smell)
   - Ideas for incorporating the product into local cuisine
