Lesson Plan: Food Preservation in Timor-Leste - Canning and Drying Foods

Resources:
See activities and Readings folder

Duration: 120 minutes

Objectives:
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Understand the principles of food preservation through canning and drying
2. Identify local foods in Timor-Leste suitable for canning and drying
3. Describe the process of water bath canning and solar drying
4. Recognize the benefits and safety considerations of these preservation methods
5. Demonstrate basic techniques for canning and drying foods

Materials:
- Whiteboard and markers
- Samples of canned and dried foods (if available)
- Pictures of canning and drying processes and equipment
- Canning equipment (jars, lids, canner, tongs, funnel)
- Ingredients for demonstration (fruits or vegetables, salt, sugar)
- Materials for a simple solar dryer (wooden frame, mesh, clear plastic)
- Handouts with canning and drying guidelines
- pH strips (if available)

I. Introduction (15 minutes)
   A. Define food preservation and its importance in Timor-Leste
   B. Brief history of traditional food preservation methods in Timor-Leste
   C. Benefits of canning and drying in tropical climates

II. Local Foods Suitable for Preservation (15 minutes)
    A. Fruits: papaya, mango, pineapple, banana
    B. Vegetables: tomatoes, green beans, peppers
    C. Fish and meats
    D. Discuss seasonality and abundance of these foods

III. Principles of Canning (20 minutes)
     A. How canning preserves food
     B. Types of canning: water bath vs. pressure canning
     C. Foods suitable for water bath canning (high-acid foods)
     D. Equipment needed for canning
     E. Safety considerations and preventing botulism

IV. Water Bath Canning Process (25 minutes)
    A. Preparing jars and lids
    B. Preparing food for canning
    C. Filling jars and removing air bubbles
    D. Processing jars in boiling water bath
    E. Cooling and storing canned foods
    F. Demonstration: Canning a local fruit (e.g., papaya jam)

V. Principles of Food Drying (15 minutes)
   A. How drying preserves food
   B. Methods of drying: sun drying vs. solar drying
   C. Foods suitable for drying in Timor-Leste
   D. Advantages of solar drying over traditional sun drying

VI. Solar Drying Process (20 minutes)
    A. Constructing a simple solar dryer
    B. Preparing foods for drying
    C. Proper placement and care during drying
    D. Testing for dryness and storing dried foods
    E. Demonstration: Setting up foods in a solar dryer

VII. Benefits and Challenges (10 minutes)
     A. Extending food availability beyond seasons
     B. Reducing food waste
     C. Potential for income generation
     D. Challenges: equipment availability, climate considerations

VIII. Hands-on Activity (20 minutes)
      A. Divide students into two groups
      B. One group practices filling and sealing canning jars
      C. Other group prepares fruits or vegetables for solar drying
      D. Groups explain their process and expected outcomes

IX. Conclusion and Discussion (10 minutes)
    A. Recap key points about canning and drying foods
    B. Discuss potential for implementing these methods in Timor-Leste
    C. Address any questions from students

X. Assignment
   Students to experiment with drying a local food at home using a simple solar drying method. The report should include:
   - Chosen food and reasoning
   - Description of the drying method used
   - Observations during the drying process
   - Final product quality and storage method
   - Ideas for using the dried food in local cuisine
