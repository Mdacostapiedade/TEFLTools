# Lesson Plan: Basics of Data Analytics and Metrics

## Duration: 1 week (5 sessions, 2 hours each)

### Session 1: Introduction to Data Analytics

#### Objectives:
- Understand the concept and importance of data analytics
- Learn about different types of data analytics

#### Materials:
- Computers with spreadsheet software and basic statistical software
- Sample datasets related to Timor-Leste

#### Activities:
1. (20 min) Introduction to data analytics: definition, importance, and applications
2. (30 min) Types of data analytics: descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, prescriptive
3. (40 min) Case study analysis: How data analytics is used in decision-making
4. (25 min) Hands-on practice: Identifying types of analytics in Timor-Leste scenarios
5. (5 min) Q&A and wrap-up

#### Homework:
Research and write a brief report on how data analytics could benefit a specific sector in Timor-Leste

### Session 2: Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and Metrics

#### Objectives:
- Understand the concept of KPIs and metrics
- Learn to identify and define relevant metrics for different scenarios

#### Activities:
1. (20 min) Review of previous session and homework
2. (30 min) Introduction to KPIs and metrics: definition, importance, characteristics
3. (30 min) SMART criteria for defining effective metrics
4. (35 min) Hands-on practice: Defining KPIs for a Timor-Leste development project
5. (5 min) Q&A and wrap-up

#### Homework:
Develop a set of KPIs for measuring the success of a local business in Timor-Leste

### Session 3: Data Collection and Preparation

#### Objectives:
- Learn about different data collection methods
- Understand the importance of data quality and preparation

#### Activities:
1. (20 min) Review of previous session and homework
2. (30 min) Data collection methods: surveys, observations, existing databases
3. (30 min) Data quality dimensions: accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness
4. (35 min) Hands-on practice: Cleaning and preparing a messy dataset from Timor-Leste
5. (5 min) Q&A and wrap-up

#### Homework:
Design a data collection plan for gathering information about education in a Timorese community

### Session 4: Basic Statistical Analysis

#### Objectives:
- Understand and calculate basic descriptive statistics
- Learn to interpret statistical results in context

#### Activities:
1. (20 min) Review of previous session and homework
2. (30 min) Descriptive statistics: mean, median, mode, standard deviation
3. (30 min) Introduction to inferential statistics: hypothesis testing, p-values
4. (35 min) Hands-on practice: Analyzing a dataset on Timor-Leste's economic indicators
5. (5 min) Q&A and wrap-up

#### Homework:
Perform a basic statistical analysis on a dataset related to health outcomes in Timor-Leste

### Session 5: Interpreting and Communicating Results

#### Objectives:
- Learn to interpret analytical results
- Understand how to effectively communicate findings to stakeholders

#### Activities:
1. (20 min) Review of previous session and homework
2. (30 min) Interpreting analytical results: drawing conclusions, recognizing limitations
3. (30 min) Effective communication of data insights: reports, presentations, dashboards
4. (35 min) Hands-on practice: Creating a brief report on Timor-Leste's development progress
5. (5 min) Final Q&A and module wrap-up

#### Homework:
Prepare a presentation summarizing the key findings from your analysis of Timor-Leste's health outcomes

### Assessment:
- Daily homework assignments
- In-class participation and hands-on exercises
- Final project: Comprehensive data analytics project

### Final Project Guidelines:
1. Choose a relevant issue or sector in Timor-Leste (e.g., education, healthcare, agriculture)
2. Develop a data analytics plan including:
   - Definition of the problem and objectives
   - Identification of relevant KPIs and metrics
   - Data collection and preparation strategy
   - Basic statistical analysis of the data
   - Interpretation of results and recommendations
3. Create a report and presentation summarizing your findings and recommendations
4. Present your project to the class, explaining your methodology and conclusions

### Additional Resources:
- Books: "Data Science for Business" by Foster Provost and Tom Fawcett
- Online courses: Coursera, edX courses on data analytics
- Websites: KDnuggets, Analytics Vidhya
- Government and NGO reports on Timor-Leste for practice datasets
