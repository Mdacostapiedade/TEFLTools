# Support Material for Digestive and Urinary Systems Lesson

## 1. Key Vocabulary List with Definitions

- **Peristalsis**: Wave-like muscle contractions that move food through the digestive tract
- **Enzymes**: Biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in digestion
- **Absorption**: The process by which nutrients pass from the digestive tract into the bloodstream
- **Nephron**: The functional unit of the kidney where blood filtration and urine formation occur
- **Filtration**: The process of separating solid particles from a fluid by passing it through a filter
- **Homeostasis**: The maintenance of a stable internal environment in the body
- **Bolus**: A small rounded mass of chewed food
- **Villi**: Tiny finger-like projections in the small intestine that increase surface area for absorption
- **Bile**: A greenish-yellow fluid produced by the liver to help emulsify fats
- **Glomerulus**: A network of capillaries in the nephron where blood filtration begins
- **Reabsorption**: The process of reclaiming useful substances from the filtrate in the nephron
- **Secretion**: The process of removing additional wastes from the blood into the nephron tubules

## 5. Tips for Teachers on Potential Challenges and How to Address Them

1. Challenge: Students struggling with complex terminology
   - Tip: Create a word wall with key terms and their simplified definitions. Encourage students to use the terms in context during discussions.

2. Challenge: Difficulty visualizing internal organ structures
   - Tip: Use a combination of 3D models, diagrams, and online interactive tools. Consider using analogy models (e.g., hose for intestines, filter for kidneys) to explain functions.

3. Challenge: Connecting anatomy to physiological processes
   - Tip: Use flow charts and animations to show step-by-step processes. Incorporate hands-on activities like the digestive system food model to make concepts more tangible.

4. Challenge: Relating content to local health issues
   - Tip: Integrate case studies based on common digestive and urinary problems in Timor-Leste. Invite a local healthcare worker as a guest speaker to discuss relevant health challenges.

5. Challenge: Maintaining engagement during lecture portions
   - Tip: Break up lectures with quick pair-share activities or "think-pair-share" moments. Use real-life examples and questions to prompt critical thinking.

6. Challenge: Ensuring proper use of lab equipment
   - Tip: Provide clear, step-by-step instructions for lab activities. Demonstrate techniques before students begin and circulate to provide hands-on guidance as needed.
