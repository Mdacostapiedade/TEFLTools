# Lesson Plan: Introduction to Timorese Food Systems and Cultural Context

## 1. Resources Needed

- Large world map and map of Timor-Leste
- Sticky notes and markers
- Projector and computer for presentations
- Handouts with timeline of Timorese history
- Sample Timorese ingredients or food items (if available)
- "The Living History of Timor-Leste" by Geoffrey C. Gunn (excerpts)

## 2. Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Locate Timor-Leste on a map and describe its geographical features
- Outline key events in Timorese history that have influenced its food culture
- Identify at least 5 traditional Timorese ingredients or dishes
- Explain how colonialism and conflict have impacted Timorese foodways

## 3. Warm-up Activity (15 minutes)

- Students write down 3 dishes that represent their personal food history on sticky notes
- In small groups, students share their dishes and discuss how they reflect their cultural background
- Class discussion on how food relates to personal and cultural identity

## 4. Pre-teaching Key Vocabulary (10 minutes)

Introduce and explain key terms:
- Foodways
- Colonialism
- Indigenous cuisine
- Food sovereignty
- Culinary heritage

## 5. Presentation of Main Lesson Content (40 minutes)

### Historical Overview of Timor-Leste
- Brief presentation on Timor-Leste's geography and pre-colonial history
- Timeline activity: Students arrange key historical events on a large timeline

### Traditional Timorese Cuisine
- Presentation on staple ingredients and dishes
- Discussion of regional variations in Timorese cuisine

### Impact of Colonialism and Conflict
- Lecture on Portuguese and Indonesian influences on Timorese food
- Discussion of how conflict has affected food production and access

## 6. Practice Activities (30 minutes)

### Timorese Food Geography Mapping
- In groups, students create a map of Timor-Leste, marking:
  * Major agricultural regions
  * Traditional dishes associated with different areas
  * Introduced crops and their origins

## 7. Production Task (20 minutes)

### Food History Comparison
- Students create a Venn diagram comparing their personal food history (from warm-up) with traditional Timorese foodways
- Class discussion on similarities and differences

## 8. Wrap-up and Review (15 minutes)

- Quick quiz on key facts about Timorese geography and history
- Group reflection on how food systems reflect broader historical and cultural contexts

## 9. Homework Assignment

- Read selected excerpts from "The Living History of Timor-Leste" by Geoffrey C. Gunn
- Write a 500-word reflection on how Timorese food culture has been shaped by its history

## 10. Key Vocabulary Definitions

- Foodways: The cultural, social, and economic practices relating to the production and consumption of food
- Colonialism: The practice of acquiring political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically
- Indigenous cuisine: The traditional dishes and cooking practices of the native inhabitants of a region
- Food sovereignty: The right of peoples to healthy and culturally appropriate food produced through ecologically sound and sustainable methods
- Culinary heritage: The collective food traditions and practices passed down through generations in a culture
