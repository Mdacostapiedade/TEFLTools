# Lesson Plan: Traditional Timorese Weaving Techniques

## 1. Resources Needed

- Backstrap looms (1 per 2-3 students)
- Assorted yarns (cotton and local plant fibers)
- Shuttles, heddles, and other basic weaving tools
- Visual aids (posters or digital slides) showing traditional Timorese motifs
- Samples of tais textiles
- Notebooks and pencils for students
- Invited local master weaver for demonstration

## 2. Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Identify at least three traditional Timorese weaving patterns and motifs
- Explain the cultural significance of tais weaving in Timorese society
- Set up and operate a basic backstrap loom
- Begin weaving a simple tais-inspired project

## 3. Warm-up Activity (10 minutes)

- Display various tais samples and ask students to observe and discuss in pairs:
  - What colors do they see?
  - What patterns or motifs can they identify?
  - What do they think these designs might represent?
- Have pairs share their observations with the class

## 4. Pre-teaching Key Vocabulary (10 minutes)

Introduce and explain key terms:
- Tais
- Backstrap loom
- Warp
- Weft
- Shuttle
- Heddle
- Motif

Use visual aids and actual weaving tools to demonstrate each term.

## 5. Presentation of Main Lesson Content (30 minutes)

- Brief lecture on the history and cultural significance of tais weaving in Timor-Leste
- Explanation of common Timorese motifs and their meanings
- Introduction to the backstrap loom:
  - Parts of the loom
  - How it works
  - Advantages and limitations
- Demonstration by local master weaver:
  - Setting up the backstrap loom
  - Basic weaving techniques
  - Creating simple motifs

## 6. Practice Activities (40 minutes)

- Students work in pairs to set up a backstrap loom
- Instructor and master weaver circulate to assist and provide feedback
- Once looms are set up, students practice basic weaving techniques

## 7. Production Task (30 minutes)

- Students begin a simple tais-inspired project on their backstrap looms
- Encourage students to incorporate at least one traditional Timorese motif in their design

## 8. Wrap-up and Review (15 minutes)

- Class discussion:
  - What challenges did students face in setting up and using the backstrap loom?
  - How does the backstrap loom differ from the frame looms used in previous lessons?
  - What insights did they gain about traditional Timorese weaving?
- Quick review of key vocabulary and motifs learned

## 9. Homework Assignment

- Research and sketch three traditional Timorese motifs not covered in class
- Write a short paragraph explaining the cultural significance of each motif
- Continue working on the tais-inspired project (if looms can be taken home or additional class time will be provided)

## 10. Key Vocabulary Definitions

- Tais: Traditional Timorese woven textile, often used for ceremonial purposes
- Backstrap loom: A simple loom where one end is attached to a fixed object and the other to the weaver's body
- Warp: The vertical threads on a loom
- Weft: The horizontal threads woven through the warp
- Shuttle: A tool used to carry the weft thread across the warp
- Heddle: A looped wire or cord used to separate and guide warp threads
- Motif: A decorative design or pattern used in weaving
